How to solve

After selecting a grid you can follow a few simple to steps to solve the Binary Puzzle

1. An easy first step in solving is to check for two-in-a-rows of numbers (see example). Since 3-in-a-rows are prohibited you can always fill in the squares after and before the three in a row in its column/row
2. The no 3-in-a-rows rule can also be applied to situations with two identical numbers surronding a blank (see example)



3. Another rule postulates that there may only be 3 of each number in any row or column, so if you have only one square left in a row or column you can work out which number it must be.



4. Finally if you've exhausted all other methods and some squares are still empty, and advanced stratergy involves the fact that no two rows or columns can be the same. So if filling in a squrare with a certain number would make one a row/column the same as another, that square must be filled in with the inverse of that number.

Happy Solving
